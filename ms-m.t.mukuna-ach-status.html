❓ Why Is This Pending?

The bank cannot legally release your funds without OCC approval. The OCC is the primary federal regulator responsible for supervising all national banks. Before a bank can release funds exceeding $500,000 from flagged entities, the OCC requires a final compliance review to ensure:

  • All required reports have been properly filed with FinCEN, IRS, OFAC, and other federal agencies
  • Anti-Money Laundering (AML) protocols have been fully satisfied
  • Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements have been met
  • Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) compliance is confirmed

Without this clearance, the bank cannot release your funds — even though your money is already at the bank and ready for disbursement.

Why visit in person? The OCC requires an in-person visit to complete the following final steps on-site:
  • • Physically stamp and execute the final compliance affidavit — The original OCC stamp and "wet ink" signature must be applied to the final affidavit in person.
  • • Witness the final funds release authorization — The final authorization must be signed in the presence of an OCC compliance officer.
  • • Receive the original OCC compliance clearance certificate (Form OCC-808) — The original physical certificate is issued on-site. A digital copy will be transmitted to the bank electronically.
  • • Conclude the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) Final Review — The OCC officer physically closes the BSA file and signs the final closure memo.
  • • Provide physical signature on OFAC certification — OFAC clearance requires a physical signature from both the beneficiary and the OCC officer.
  • • Complete the final interview with the OCC examiner — A brief final interview is required to confirm all source of funds documentation is consistent with reports submitted.
  • • Collect the official OCC compliance seal — The official OCC compliance seal is applied to the final release documents in person.
Note: In normal circumstances, the OCC only handles compliance reviews in person. Beneficiaries are required to visit the headquarters office in Washington, D.C. without any fee.
